Beginner Bowling

There are many paths you can take when learning to bowl, so starting with a strong foundation is important.          

Grip

Later on, you can change your grip to suit the bowling type which you prefer, but to begin it's best to start with your fingers on the seam.

Line and Length

The line (left or right) and the length (forward and backward) have an impact upon the shots a batter can play. For a beginner, it may be simple to try and be consistant on one line and length. The best place to start is the top of off stump, if the batter is right handed, the off stump is on the left, if the batter is left handed, the right.

Angle 

When bowling you have the choice of bowling around the wicket, with the arm you bowl with furthest away from the stumps, or over the wicket, with the arm going over the top of the stumps, the easiest place to start is bowling over the wicket.    

Action and Release 

Unlike sports like baseball, there are restrictions upon how you can bowl the ball. One of the most important is that when delivering the ball, the bowling arm must not be bent at the elbow.

Another important consideration is the front foot, you can land it on or behind the front (or popping) crease, but never in front.

Recovery

In match play, everyone bowls bad balls that may be called a wide or no ball, or get hit for a boundary. In these conditions what is important is the recovery, if you have just bowled a poor ball, make the next ball your best and land it in the right area, you can still take that wicket!
